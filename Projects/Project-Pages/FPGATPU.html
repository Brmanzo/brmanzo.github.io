FPGA-Based TPU

Pictured are Bradley Manzo, Kent Cassidy, Austin York, and Gurkirat Dhillon


For my senior design at UC Davis in 2023, I led a team of four in designing a TPU in hardware using an FPGA. During our first quarter of development, we prototyped and verified our 8x8 systolic array in verilog on the DE1-SoC development board. We then developed our data orchestration and control logic in C which ran on the ARM-based Hard Processing System. We faced many challenges with the limited resources of the FPGA, but we were able to accomodate for these limitations in software. In the end we implemented a fully functional TPU capable of tiling and sparse Matrix multiplication up to 4096x4096, and later convolutional workloads at batch level. We demonstrated that our TPU was faster than our software benchmark at high sizes and high sparsity and received the outstanding senior design award for our class.
